Arabic 10 Forms

Form 1: فَعَلَ (fa'ala)

This form denotes the fundamental or standard meaning of the root verb, akin to the English infinitive. For instance: كَتَبَ (kataba) - He wrote. Other examples include: قَرَأَ (qara'a) - He read, رَكِبَ (rakiba) - He rode, شَرِبَ (shariba) - He drank, أَكَلَ (akala) - He ate, نَامَ (nama) - He slept, مَشَى (masha) - He walked.

Form 2: فَعَّلَ (fa'ala)

The second form intensifies or causativizes the action expressed in form 1. For example, transforming علم in form 1 (to know) into علّم in form 2 (to teach). Similarly, كسّر in form 2 (to smash) intensifies the root verb كسر (to break).

Form 3: فاعَلَ (faa'ala)

Form 3 typically denotes a transitive action or a relationship with an object or another entity. For instance, the root verb for "to be safe" سلم in form 3 becomes "to make peace with someone" سالم. Another example is the transformation of "to become known" علن in form 1 into "to disclose something to someone" عالن in form 3.

Form 4: أفْعَلَ (af'ala)

Form 4 indicates a causative or transitive action stemming from form 1. For example, instead of simply "to write" كتب in form 1, form 4 transforms it into "to dictate" أكتب. Similarly, "to be good" صلح in form 1 becomes "to put in order" أصلح in form 4. This form can also express actions related to different times of the day.

Form 5: تَفَعَّلَ (tafa'ala)

Form 5 represents a reflexive or passive action of form 1 or 2. For example, transforming رقب in form 1 (to observe) into ترقب in form 5 (to anticipate). Also, "to be destroyed" تنقض in form 5 contrasts with "to destroy" نقض in form 1.

Form 6: تَفاعَلَ (tafa'ala)

The sixth form denotes a reflexive or passive action related to form 3. For instance, the verb for "to be fast" سرع in form 1 becomes "to rush or hurry" تسارع in form 6. Similarly, "to work" عمل in form 1 transforms into "to do business with" تعامل in form 6.

Form 7: اِنْفَعَلَ (infa'ala)

Form 7 indicates the passive voice of form 1. For example, transforming "to tie" عقد in form 1 into "to be knit together" انعقد in form 7. Similarly, "to break" فجر becomes "to explode or detonate" انفجر in form 7.

Form 8: اِفْتَعَلَ (ifta'ala)

Form 8 conveys reflexive nuances and often implies doing something with attention to detail or intentionally. For instance, "to hear" سمع in form 1 becomes "to listen" استمع in form 8. Likewise, "to acknowledge" اعترف in form 8 adds a layer of intention compared to "to know" عرف in form 1.

Form 9: اِفْعَلَّ (if'alla)

This form, although rare, denotes actions related to acquiring a color or a defect. Examples include "to turn red" احمر and "to be twisted" اعوج.

Form 10: اِسْتَفْعَلَ (istaf'ala)

Form 10 implies seeking an action or quality expressed in form 1. It can also signify considering something to be such. For instance, transforming "to increase" زاد in form 1 into "to try to achieve more" استزاد in form 10. Similarly, "to be safe" سلم in form 1 becomes "to surrender" استسلم in form 10.
